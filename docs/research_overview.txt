MISSION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The FEAST Lab investigates the complex relationships between dietary patterns, metabolic health,
and chronic disease. We combine large-scale nutritional epidemiology with AI-driven analysis
to understand what — and how — we eat, and what it means for long-term health outcomes.

Our work spans basic nutritional science, public health research, and applied AI/ML for
automated dietary assessment. We are committed to open science and community-engaged research,
with a particular focus on underserved populations in rural Missouri.

ACTIVE RESEARCH AREAS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. DIET & METABOLIC HEALTH
   Study of how macronutrient composition, meal timing, and dietary patterns affect insulin
   sensitivity, lipid profiles, and cardiometabolic risk across diverse populations.
   Current projects:
   - MU Cohort Study (n=3,200): 5-year longitudinal dietary tracking and metabolic outcomes
   - Low-carbohydrate vs. Mediterranean diet RCT (n=180, recruiting)
   - Meal timing and circadian rhythm study (n=60, active)
   Lead researcher: Dr. Krishnaswami + Dr. James Okafor (postdoc)

2. GUT MICROBIOME & NUTRITION
   Characterizing how dietary fiber, fermented foods, and ultra-processed food consumption
   shape gut microbial communities and systemic inflammation markers.
   Current projects:
   - Fiber intervention study: 8-week high-fiber diet and microbiome response (n=95, active)
   - Ultra-processed food consumption and microbiome diversity (n=400 cross-sectional, analysis phase)
   - Fermented food RCT: kefir, kimchi, kombucha effects on gut diversity (n=120, recruiting)
   Lead researcher: Dr. Priya Nair (postdoc) + PhD student Marcus Webb

3. FOOD ENVIRONMENT MAPPING
   GIS-based analysis of food access, food desert prevalence, and the relationship between
   neighborhood food environments and diet quality in urban and rural Missouri.
   Current projects:
   - Missouri Food Access Atlas (statewide GIS dataset, publication in prep)
   - Rural food desert intervention study: mobile market impact on diet quality (n=200)
   - School food environment and childhood obesity in Boone County (in review, Pediatrics)
   Lead researcher: PhD student Alicia Fontaine + Dr. Krishnaswami

4. AI-POWERED DIETARY ANALYSIS
   Using computer vision, NLP, and large language models to automate 24-hour dietary recall
   analysis, food frequency questionnaire processing, and nutrition label parsing at scale.
   Current projects:
   - FoodVision: CNN-based food image recognition and portion estimation (87% accuracy on 200-class dataset)
   - AutoRecall: LLM-assisted 24-hour dietary recall processing (reduces analyst time by 70%)
   - RAG-powered lab assistant: vector search across all lab documents for instant Q&A
   Lead researcher: Sresta Ganji (AI Full Stack Engineer) + PhD student Leo Chang
