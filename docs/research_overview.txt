ABOUT THE LAB
The FEAST Lab (Food Engineering And Sustainable Technologies) is a transdisciplinary research group at the University of Missouri working at the interface of science, engineering, and technology to address Hidden Hunger — micronutrient deficiency affecting over 2 billion people globally.

MISSION
To advance breakthrough food and bioprocessing technologies that position food as medicine, strengthen food and nutrition security, and translate research innovations into real-world impact.

VISION
Building a sustainable and nutritious future for all through circular food systems. The FEAST Lab aims to transform food loss into value, nourish people, and sustain the planet.

WHY FEAST RESEARCH?
Our world produces enough food to nourish its entire population, yet nearly one-third is lost or wasted each year. At the same time, over two billion people suffer from hidden hunger caused by micronutrient deficiencies, contributing to the global double burden of malnutrition (wasting/stunting AND overweight/obesity). The FEAST Research Group develops sustainable food systems innovations to address these interconnected challenges and advance both food and nutrition security.

THREE CORE RESEARCH AREAS

1. FOOD FORTIFICATION
Fortification of foods with multiple micronutrients to address hidden hunger (micronutrient deficiencies).
- Double and triple fortification of salt with iron, iodine, and folic acid
- Quadruple fortification of salt including Vitamin B12
- Microencapsulation of ferrous fumarate for scale-up of double fortification
- Vitamin B12 encapsulation in soymilk powder and spray-dried conditions analysis
- Beetroot processing for enhanced iron retention
- Technology for triple fortification of salt with iron, iodine, and zinc oxide using extrusion
- Improving lives through new double fortification of salt technology (deployed in Uttar Pradesh, India reaching 24 million people)

2. INNOVATIVE TECHNOLOGIES
Scaling-up strategies and designing nano-micro-macro scale encapsulation systems for micronutrient delivery.
- Spray drying and microencapsulation optimization using response surface methodology (RSM)
- Green nanotechnology — synthesis of gold nanoparticles from grape waste (Vitis vinifera)
- Beta-cyclodextrin molecular inclusion of catechin for nano-encapsulation
- Microwave-assisted extraction of phenolic antioxidants from grape seeds
- In-vitro release profiling of micronutrients in food media
- Gold nanofluids from catechin synthesis and characterization

3. SUSTAINABLE FOOD SYSTEMS ENGINEERING
Creating a circular bioeconomy by upcycling food loss/waste and recovering micronutrients from underutilized native plants.
- Sustainable zero-waste processing system for soybeans and soy by-product valorization
- Upcycling of Greek yogurt acid whey with kodo and proso millets into complementary food powders
- Circular economy approach: spray-dried acid whey encapsulated in millet matrix
- American elderberry (Sambucus nigra) spray drying and microencapsulation for phytochemical-rich ingredients
- Pawpaw (Asimina triloba) physical characterization from eight North American cultivars
- Black walnut (Juglans nigra) — quantification of vitamins, minerals, and amino acids
- Ancient grains: physical and structural characterization of millets, sorghum, and amaranth
- Mango waste upcycling to support circular economy in East Africa
- Natural whitening alternative from upcycled food waste (acid whey) and underutilized grains

FOCUS: ONE HEALTH — WOMEN AND CHILDREN
The FEAST Lab's research is centered on SDG 2 — Zero Hunger, supporting UN Sustainable Development Goals including SDG 12 (Responsible Consumption) and SDG 3 (Good Health). Work spans USA, India, Canada, and Kenya.
