FEAST LAB — FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

GENERAL QUESTIONS

Q: What is the FEAST Lab?
A: FEAST stands for Food Engineering And Sustainable Technologies. The FEAST Lab is a transdisciplinary research group at the University of Missouri–Columbia, led by Dr. Kiruba Krishnaswamy. We work at the interface of science, engineering, and technology to address Hidden Hunger (micronutrient deficiency) through circular food systems innovation.

Q: Where is the FEAST Lab located?
A: The FEAST Lab is located at 244 Agricultural Engineering Building, University of Missouri, Columbia, MO 65211.

Q: How can I contact the FEAST Lab?
A: Email Dr. Kiruba Krishnaswamy at krishnaswamyk@umsystem.edu. You can also visit the lab website at feastlabs.org.

Q: What does the FEAST Lab research?
A: The FEAST Lab focuses on three core areas:
1. Food Fortification — fortifying foods with multiple micronutrients to address hidden hunger
2. Innovative Technologies — nano-micro-macro scale encapsulation systems for micronutrient delivery
3. Sustainable Food Systems Engineering — creating circular bioeconomy by upcycling food waste and recovering micronutrients from underutilized native plants

Q: What is "Hidden Hunger"?
A: Hidden Hunger refers to micronutrient deficiency — a lack of essential vitamins and minerals (like iron, iodine, folic acid, Vitamin B12) in the diet. It affects over 2 billion people worldwide and contributes to the double burden of malnutrition, which includes both wasting/stunting and overweight/obesity.

Q: What is the FEAST Lab's mission?
A: To advance breakthrough food and bioprocessing technologies that position food as medicine, strengthen food and nutrition security, and translate research innovations into real-world impact.

JOINING THE LAB

Q: How can I join the FEAST Lab?
A: The FEAST Lab is currently accepting applications from highly passionate and self-motivated Post-Doctoral Fellows, PhD students, and Undergraduate students. Email krishnaswamyk@umsystem.edu with your application materials.

Q: What should I include in my application?
A: Please provide a full CV including:
- Previous education history with cumulative GPA/percentages
- Awards and funding received
- Skills and technologies
- Publications (if any)
- Conference presentations, posters, and patents
- References

Q: What is the selection process?
A: Students who are shortlisted will be invited for a Zoom interview. Note that more than 85% of students shortlisted for first-round MS/PhD interviews have at least 1-2 manuscripts published in peer-reviewed journals. Students are highly encouraged to apply for external awards and fellowships.

Q: Can I apply through either COE or CAFNR?
A: Yes! Dr. Krishnaswamy has a joint appointment in the Department of Chemical and Biomedical Engineering (College of Engineering - COE) and the Division of Food, Nutrition and Exercise Sciences (College of Agriculture, Food and Natural Resources - CAFNR) at the University of Missouri. Interested students can apply through either college.

Q: What kind of research would I do as a student in the FEAST Lab?
A: Students in the FEAST Lab work on projects including:
- Spray drying and microencapsulation of bioactives
- Food fortification with micronutrients
- Upcycling food industry waste (acid whey, elderberry pomace, mango waste)
- Characterization of underutilized crops (millets, sorghum, pawpaw, elderberry)
- Green nanotechnology for food applications
- Food safety and complementary food development

Q: Is funding available for graduate students?
A: The FEAST Lab has active NSF and USDA-funded projects. Funded positions may be available. Please contact Dr. Krishnaswamy directly to inquire about current openings.

RESEARCH IMPACT

Q: What is the real-world impact of FEAST Lab research?
A: The FEAST Lab's double fortification of salt technology has been deployed in Uttar Pradesh, India, helping 24 million people combat iron deficiency anemia. The lab's work on elderberry, pawpaw, and agroforestry crops supports sustainable food systems in Missouri and beyond.

Q: What SDGs does the FEAST Lab support?
A: The FEAST Lab primarily supports:
- SDG 2: Zero Hunger
- SDG 12: Responsible Consumption and Production
- SDG 3: Good Health and Well-Being

Q: Does the FEAST Lab collaborate internationally?
A: Yes, the lab has worked with partners in India, Canada, Kenya, Australia, and across the USA through NSF, USDA, FAO, and NGO partnerships.
